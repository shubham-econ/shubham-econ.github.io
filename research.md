### Causes and Consequences of SC/ST Candidate Presence in Unreserved Constituencies: Evidence from Maharashtra

This paper examines the determinants and developmental consequences of Scheduled Caste (SC) and Scheduled Tribe (ST) candidates contesting from unreserved constituencies in Maharashtra.

I show that ST candidate presence is primarily driven by demographic concentration, whereas SC candidate presence depends not only on population share but also on proximity to Ambedkarite movement centers, the organizational presence of Ambedkarite political parties, and intergenerational political participation.

To estimate the consequences of candidate presence, I employ a staggered difference-in-differences design and find a positive effect on village-level literacy. Using propensity score matching and linear probability models, I further show that improvements in public goods provision—particularly ASHA worker availability, water infrastructure, and road access—are concentrated in villages exposed to SC/ST candidates across multiple elections.

The findings suggest that repeated political participation strengthens the bargaining power of marginalized communities and improves local development outcomes over time.

**Keywords:** Political Representation, Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Elections, Development, Maharashtra
