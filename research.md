## title: Research

### Causes and Consequences of SC/ST Candidate Presence in Unreserved Constituencies: Evidence from Maharashtra

**Status:** Working Paper

**Keywords:** Political Representation, Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, Elections, Development, Maharashtra

<details>
<summary><strong>Abstract</strong></summary>

A vast literature on affirmative action focuses on outcomes in constituencies reserved for Scheduled Castes (SCs) and Scheduled Tribes (STs). Whereas this paper studies SC and ST candidates contesting from unreserved constituencies in Maharashtra. It addresses two questions: What enables SC and ST candidates to effectively contest from unreserved (general) constituencies, and what consequences does their presence have for village-level development outcomes?

On the enabling side, I find that ST candidate presence is primarily driven by demographic concentration—numerical strength remains the key threshold condition. For SC candidates, however, population share alone is insufficient. Three additional factors matter: historical proximity to Ambedkarite movement centers, the organizational presence of Ambedkarite political parties, and intergenerational effects of political participation.

On the outcomes side, using a staggered difference-in-differences framework, I find a modest but statistically significant positive effect of effective SC and ST presence on village-level literacy. Extending the analysis to public goods provision through propensity score matching and linear probability models, I show that improvements in ASHA worker availability, water infrastructure, and road access are concentrated in villages exposed to SC/ST candidates across two or more elections. By contrast, single-election exposure yields no significant gains.

These findings are consistent with a mechanism of cumulative bargaining power: repeated political participation strengthens the recognition and influence of SC and ST communities, increasing the responsiveness of local officials to their demands and improving development outcomes over time.
